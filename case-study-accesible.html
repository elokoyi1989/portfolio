Story & Scope

In “Accessible Components” I set out to reimagine the Mac File Navigator’s dropdown. Though dropdowns seem simple, they can often be exclusive to keyboard and screen-reader users. My goal was to uncover a more seamless experience for all users.

Accessibility Audit

I compared dropdowns in Netflix, YouTube, and the Mac File Navigator under both mouse and keyboard use. Key findings:

  • No visible ARIA labels or skip-links, confusing screen readers.
  • Keyboard users forced to tab through every menu item.
  • Mouse focus rings missing or inconsistent.

The Redesign

Key Enhancements

  • Lifted focus ring—always visible for keyboard users.
  • First-letter navigation—type “T” to jump to “Tools.”
  • Robust ARIA labels—clear context for screen readers.

Takeaways

Good design tells a story that everyone can access. I learned about the importance of considering all angles of design and putting myself into someone else's shoes when it comes to accessibility.
