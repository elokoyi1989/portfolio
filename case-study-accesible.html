Context

In “Accessible Components,” I set out to reimagine the Mac File Navigator’s dropdown for an assignment in a design interaction course at Brown University. Though many design components, like dropdowns, can seem simple, they can often exclude keyboard and screen-reader users. My goal was to design a more seamless experience for all users.

Accessibility Audit

First, I compared dropdowns in Netflix, YouTube, and the Mac File Navigator under both mouse and keyboard use. I did this to take a more careful and intentional look at how different users with different tools interact with interfaces. I created state models to visualize the differences in interaction:

Some issues I noted:

  • Lack of detailed ARIA labels
  • Keyboard users often haf to tab through every menu item.

The Redesign

In my redesign, I experiment with different tradeoffs and design decisions. With this design, I decided to focus on improving effiency for the keyboard users.

Key Changes

  • Pre-selected Focus—jumping to the most recent action
  • First-letter navigation—type “T” to jump to “Tools.”
  • Robust ARIA labels—clearer context for screen readers.

Takeaways

Good design tells a story that everyone can access. I learned about the importance of considering all angles of design and putting myself into someone else's shoes when it comes to accessibility.
