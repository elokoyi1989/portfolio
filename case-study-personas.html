Personas and Storyboards

This case study came out of an assignment for a design interaction course at Brown University. The project investigates the design of a public transit ticket kiosk interface, focusing on how I can use personas and storyboarding to enhance usability and empathy.

Observations

First, I conducted on-site observations and interviews of users of a public transit ticket kiosk. I collected data about who users were, what frustrated them, and what they needed.

Interview Insights

I also conducted interviews with users and involved myself directly in the process of user research and qualitative analysis of how people interact with interfaces.

To bring these insights to life, I constructed empathy maps for two personas.

Reflection

Taking on a narrative based approach to design strengthened my understanding of user needs. I learned to observe and interview users with thoughtful and effective questions. Building empathy maps and storyboards reinforced a core philosophy for me, which is that design is about understanding the people behind problems.
