Responsive Redesign

Redesigning the Berkshire Hathaway homepage to be more user-friendly and accessible.

Context & Problem

Tasked with finding a poor web interface for the Brown University CS1300: Design Interaction course, I chose the notoriously bad Berkshire Hathaway homepage. My goal was to redesign it to be more user-friendly and accessible, while maintaining the brand's minimalist aesthetic.

The original site had several issues:

  • Efficiency: No search bar; links are unorganized.
  • Accessibility gaps: Missing ARIA labels (WebAIM WAVE).
  • Lack of hierarchy: All text and links look identical.

Visual Style Guide & Responsive Mockups

To give the look a little bit more style, while preserving the no-frills brand, I first created a visual style guide. I focused on a color palette, typography, and component states, then created responsive specifc mockups for mobile, tablet, and desktop.

Implementation

I built the new homepage with HTML and CSS, enhancing core weaknesses of the original site.:

  • A search bar at the top.
  • Clear heading hierarchy and organized links.
  • ARIA labels on key elements for screen-reader use.

Original Site • Redesign

Conclusion

This redesign shows that even the most minimalist, bare-bones designs can improve accessibility and responsiveness without losing its core identity. The story and brand of the Berkshire Hathaway homepage is one that is clearly important to the company, and it was important to me to preserve that while also making it more usable.
